Power monitoring that catches trouble early

We add current monitoring to pumps, motors, compressors, refrigeration, HVAC, irrigation, dust collectors, and other equipment with a motor.

  • We install a simple current sensor
  • The system learns normal power use
  • If amp draw jumps 15–20%, you get an alert
  • You schedule service before the breakdown hits

Example: “Well pump #2 — high amp draw. Schedule maintenance.”

That means Tuesday repair rates instead of Saturday night emergency prices.

FAQ

What if the internet goes down?

The system keeps working locally and stores data. When internet comes back, alerts and history sync again.

Do I need a special phone?

No. Any smartphone works. SMS is also possible for simple alerts.

Will there be a monthly subscription?

No required subscription. You own the system. Optional cloud archive can be added if needed.

I do not know what SCADA or a contactor is.

You do not need to. We translate the technical part into normal language.

How much do pilot project parts usually cost?

Typical small project hardware is around $300–$800, depending on sensors, communications, and site conditions.

Can another engineer maintain it later?

Yes. That is one of the main points. We provide proper documentation, not mystery wiring.
